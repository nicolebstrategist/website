About

My early experience with chronic allergies and skin conditions led me to question not only symptoms, but the broader imbalances shaping my life. What began as a search for physical relief evolved into a deeper investigation of daily exposures — environmental, relational and psychological — and their cumulative impact on the nervous system and identity.

Over time, I implemented structured, evidence-informed changes to reduce toxic load, strengthen boundaries and align my habits with my values. While some conditions remain, they are now managed with clarity rather than reactivity. This journey shaped the core principle of my work: lasting wellbeing is built through conscious, coherent decisions grounded in self-respect.

Purpose

To guide people in claiming authorship of their own lives through self-respect.

Vision

To promote a cultural shift from passivity and self-criticism towards self-respect as the foundation of lasting wellbeing.

Values

  • Awareness: choosing with calm and clarity.

  • Longevity: prioritising the long-term over immediacy.

My Approach

I work from a systemic perspective, addressing the broader patterns shaping your behaviour rather than isolated symptoms. This includes environment, habits, relationships, internal dialogue and cultural context. Change becomes realistic and sustainable when decisions are aligned across these domains.

Self-respect functions as the organising principle of this process. Through structured, low-tox shifts — physical, relational and psychological — reactive patterns are replaced with deliberate choices. The result is not intensity or quick transformation, but coherence, stability and long-term wellbeing.
